Question / Answer

Tigers:
1. What is the average size of a tiger?
Ans. Tigers can grow up to 3 meters (10 feet) in length and weigh between 100 to 300 kg (220 to 660 lbs), depending on the species.
2. How many subspecies of tigers exist?
Ans. There are six living subspecies: Bengal, Indochinese, Malayan, Siberian, South China, and Sumatran tigers.
3. How fast can a tiger run?
Ans. Tigers can run up to 40 mph (64 km/h) for short distances while chasing prey.
4. Do tigers climb trees?
Ans. Yes, although they are not as agile as leopards, tigers can climb trees, especially younger ones. 5. How do tigers communicate?
Ans. Tigers communicate through vocalizations like roars, growls, and chuffs, as well as scent marking and body language.
6. What is the lifespan of a tiger?
Ans. In the wild, tigers usually live between 10 to 15 years, but in captivity, they can live up to 20 years.
7. Do tigers swim?
Ans. Yes! Tigers are excellent swimmers and often enjoy cooling off in water.
8. How strong is a tiger's bite?
Ans. A tiger's bite force is around 1,050 psi (pounds per square inch), making it one of the strongest among big cats.
9. How many tigers are left in the wild?
Ans. There are fewer than 5,000 tigers remaining in the wild, mainly in Asia.
10. Why are tigers important to ecosystems?
Ans. Tigers help regulate prey populations, maintaining a balance in their habitats and contributing to biodiversity.

Lions:
1. How large can a lion grow?
• Male lions can reach up to 3 meters (10 feet) in length and weigh between 150 to 250 kg (330 to 550 lbs).
2. What makes male lions different from females?
• Male lions have a mane, which helps them appear more intimidating and offers protection during fights.
3. How fast can a lion run?
• Lions can reach speeds of up to 50 mph (80 km/h) in short bursts when hunting.
4. Do lions roar?
• Yes! Lions have one of the loudest roars among big cats, which can be heard up to 5 miles (8 km) away.
5. How do lions hunt?
• Lionesses work together to stalk and ambush prey, using teamwork to increase hunting success.
6. What is the lifespan of a lion?
• In the wild, lions live around 12 to 16 years, but they can live over 20 years in captivity.
7. Do lions have natural enemies?
• Lions don't have many predators, but they occasionally fight off hyenas, crocodiles, and rival lions.
8. How do lions communicate?
• Lions use vocalizations, scent markings, and body gestures to communicate within their pride.
9. Are lions endangered?
• Lions are considered vulnerable due to habitat loss, human conflicts, and poaching.
10. What role do lions play in their ecosystem?
• Lions regulate prey populations, helping maintain ecological balance in savannas and grasslands.
